Shrill highs, weak mids & anemic bass

I'd wanted a pair of Bose headphones for years and jumped at the chance to pick up the AE2i's when they went on sale. Although I was initially thrilled with the exceptional comfort of these headphones, the feeling was quickly replaced with shocked disbelief when I heard the unbearably shrill highs, weak mids and anemic bass while listening to a variety of music. Beyond the brutal sound, the AE2i's have a cheap, insubstantial feel to their mostly plastic construction and I got the feeling that they wouldn't hold up long with repeated use for daily commuting. Adding to the cheapness factor is an extremely thin cable and a stenciled (rather than chromed/embossed) Bose logo on the side of each can. On top of this, the iPhone mic on the cable controller doesn't work well, and voice recognition is poor at best while trying to make a call. I have several pairs of other headphones at different price points (some as low as $50) from other Brands, and all of them overshadow and outperform the AE2i's in terms of build and sound. Overall, I expected substantially more from a company of Bose's reputation. They've priced these headphones higher than comparable products from competitors -- yet deliver a shockingly poor listening and product experience. If audio quality and durability are important to you, I'd urge you to try a different pair of headphones. These are a terrible disappointment.
