4.0 out of 5 stars There is still room for improvement, but compared to previous models and competitors they are great, May 9, 2013
By 
S. Power (Austin, Texas, United States) - See all my reviews
(TOP 100 REVIEWER)    (VINE VOICE)    (REAL NAME)   
This review is from: Bose® QuietComfort® 15 Acoustic Noise Cancelling® Headphones (Electronics)
This is my third pair of Bose Quiet Comfort Headphones. I bought each previous version, upgrading each time there was a new one. These QC15 are much better than the earlier models, but there is still room for improvement. I use these about 8 hours a day in a noisy office, then at home to isolate me from the noise from TVs and other family members, and about once a week on flights. Out of those three uses they do the best at reducing airplane noise. They are tuned to almost completely remove the noise that comes from airplane engines. They are almost as good at reducing the sound of a noisy office environment, but if someone is talking on the phone very close to me I have to turn the volume up on my music to really mask their conversation. At home, they are still good at reducing background noise, but something like the television in the next room doesn't get as masked as other types of sounds unless you turn up what you are listening to.

I highly recommend this headphones for frequent fliers and for people that have a difficult time concentrating when there is background conversations or television. They are well made and do a very good job. I just look forward to Bose taking this technology to the next level. I would also consider headphones that use passive isolation since some of them do just as good a job at a lower cost than these do.
