    Specifications

    See All Specs
    The best noise cancelling headphones from Bose®. Significant advances in noise reduction make these the quietest Bose® headphones ever. Around-ear style. Additional cable with inline remote and microphone for convenient control of select Apple® products. AAA battery and carrying case included.

    Product Features

    Around-ear design

    Provides a comfortable listening experience. Earcups fold flat for easy storage.

    Acoustic Noise-canceling® technology

    Uses microphones both inside and outside each earcup to reduce unwanted noise.

    TriPort® acoustic headphone structure

    Features tiny vents in the earcups to enable a balanced audio performance.

    5' 5" cord length

    Allows you to move around and work on other tasks while listening to your music.

    3.5mm plug

    For an easy connection. Also features a detachable audio cable and airline adapter.

    Additional cable with inline remote and microphone

    For convenient control of select Apple® products.

    AAA battery (included)

    Provides up to 35 hours of playing time.

    Carrying case with accessory pouch

    For easy portability.

    What's great about it: Best Bose NC headphones yet...
    What's not so great: $300 is $300...
    IMO, these are excellent headphones... I can't say it any simpler than that. I work from home and I usually end up working late most nights. My office is right next to our bedroom and my GF usually goes to bed a good hour or 2 before I do. I just pop the QC 15's on and either plug them into my laptop or my Bose computer speakers so I can listen to iTunes or whatever... It is a win-win situation for both of us. It also helps me get more work done during the day as they help to block out all of the "distractions" one has when working from home.
    The sound quality is very impressive. They are extremely comfortable too - at least they are for me. Not too bulky on my melon. I actually have to watch getting up from my desk with them still on - that is how good of a fit they are for me.
    The only negative I can think of is that you need to watch the volume on your output source. It can leave a bit of a ringing in your ears if it's up too loud. At least that has been my experience. I guess for those not accustomed to NC technology, it can be a bit of an adjustment. And yes, they are a bit pricey, but I got them 18 months - no interest from BB...
    Not a bad deal at all.
    I would recommend this to a friend!
